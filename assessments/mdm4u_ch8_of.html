Part A: Knowledge & Understanding [15 marks]
Question 1 [2 marks]
A correlation \( r = 0.85 \) indicates a relationship that is:
Solution:
\( r = 0.85 \): positive direction, magnitude near 1 = strong linear.
Question 2 [2 marks]
For \( r = -0.6 \), what proportion of variation in \( y \) is explained by \( x \)?
Solution:
\( r^2 = 0.36 = 36\% \).
Question 3 [2 marks]
A regression line is \( \hat{y} = 12 - 1.5x \). Predict \( \hat{y} \) when \( x = 4 \).
Solution:
\( \hat{y} = 12 - 1.5(4) = 12 - 6 = 6 \).
Question 4 [2 marks]
A residual is calculated by:
Solution:
Residual = observed - predicted = \( y - \hat{y} \).
Question 5 [3 marks]
Given \( \bar{x} = 10, \bar{y} = 50, s_x = 4, s_y = 12, r = 0.5 \). Find the regression slope.
Solution:
\( b_1 = r\dfrac{s_y}{s_x} = 0.5 \cdot \dfrac{12}{4} = 0.5 \cdot 3 = 1.5 \).
Question 6 [2 marks]
For Q5, the regression intercept is:
Solution:
\( b_0 = \bar{y} - b_1 \bar{x} = 50 - 1.5(10) = 50 - 15 = 35 \).
Question 7 [2 marks]
"Children's reading scores increase with their shoe size." This is most likely:
Solution:
Both variables increase with age — the lurking variable is age. Common cause.
