Q1
Find \(f'(x)\) for \(f(x)=3x^4-2x^2+x\) at \(x=1\).
Answer:
Solution:
\(f'(x)=12x^3-4x+1\). At \(x=1\): \(12-4+1=9\)
Q2
If \(y=(2x+1)(x^2-3)\), find \(y'\) at \(x=1\). Use the product rule.
Answer:
Solution:
\(y'=2(x^2-3)+(2x+1)(2x)=2x^2-6+4x^2+2x=6x^2+2x-6\). At \(x=1\): \(6+2-6=2\). Wait: \(y'=2(1-3)+(3)(2)=(-4)+6=2\). Hmm let me redo: \(f=2x+1,g=x^2-3\). \(f'=2,g'=2x\). \(y'=2(x^2-3)+(2x+1)(2x)\). At \(x=1\): \(2(-2)+3(2)=-4+6=2\).
Q3
The chain rule is used when:
Solution:
The chain rule applies to compositions: \(\frac{d}{dx}f(g(x))=f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x)\)
