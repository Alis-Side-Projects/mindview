1. Special Relativity

Einstein's Postulates:
1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames.
2. The speed of light in a vacuum (c) is the same for all observers, regardless of their motion.
γ = 1/√(1 - v²/c²)   (Lorentz factor)
Time dilation: Δt = γΔt₀   |   Length contraction: L = L₀/γ
Mass-energy: E = mc²   |   Relativistic energy: E = γmc²

2. Quantum Mechanics

E = hf   |   λ = h/p = h/(mv)   (de Broglie wavelength)
ΔxΔp ≥ h/(4π)   (Heisenberg uncertainty principle)
Wave-Particle Duality: All matter exhibits both wave and particle properties. Light can behave as a wave (interference, diffraction) or as particles (photoelectric effect). Electrons can behave as particles (cathode rays) or waves (electron diffraction).

📝 Worksheet — Strand E

1. [K/U — 3 marks] A spacecraft travels at 0.80c relative to Earth.

a) Calculate the Lorentz factor γ.

b) If a clock on the ship measures 10.0 hours, how much time passes on Earth?

c) If the spacecraft is 100 m long in its rest frame, what length does an Earth observer measure?

2. [K/U — 2 marks] Calculate the de Broglie wavelength of an electron (m = 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg) moving at 1.0 × 10⁶ m/s.

3. [Thinking — 4 marks] In nuclear fission, a uranium-235 nucleus absorbs a neutron and splits into barium-141, krypton-92, and 3 neutrons. The total mass of the products is 0.186 u less than the reactants (1 u = 1.66 × 10⁻²⁷ kg).

a) Calculate the mass defect in kg.

b) Calculate the energy released using E = mc².

c) Explain why nuclear reactions release so much more energy than chemical reactions.

4. [Communication — 3 marks] Explain the concept of wave-particle duality. How does the double-slit experiment demonstrate this when performed with individual electrons? What does this tell us about the nature of matter?

5. [Application — 4 marks] The "twin paradox": One twin stays on Earth while the other travels to a star 4.0 light-years away at 0.90c and returns.

a) Calculate γ for 0.90c.

b) How long does the trip take according to Earth? (Total: there and back)

c) How long does the trip take according to the traveling twin?

d) How much younger is the traveling twin upon return?
