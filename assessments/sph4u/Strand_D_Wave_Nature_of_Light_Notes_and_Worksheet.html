1. Wave Properties of Light

c = fλ   |   c = 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s
nλ = d sinθ (constructive, double slit)   |   (n+½)λ = d sinθ (destructive)
Young's Double Slit Experiment demonstrated that light behaves as a wave. When monochromatic light passes through two narrow slits, an interference pattern of bright and dark fringes appears on a screen. Bright fringes = constructive interference. Dark fringes = destructive interference.

2. Diffraction

Single slit: mλ = w sinθ (minima)   |   Diffraction grating: nλ = d sinθ (maxima)

4. Photoelectric Effect

E = hf   |   E_k = hf - W   |   h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
Photoelectric Effect: Light ejects electrons from a metal surface only if the frequency exceeds the threshold frequency (f₀). Increasing intensity increases the number of electrons, NOT their energy. This cannot be explained by wave theory — it requires treating light as particles (photons).

📝 Worksheet — Strand D

1. [K/U — 3 marks] Monochromatic light of wavelength 550 nm passes through a double slit with slit separation 0.10 mm. A screen is 2.0 m away.

a) Calculate the angle to the first bright fringe.

b) Calculate the distance from the central maximum to the first bright fringe on the screen.

2. [Thinking — 4 marks] Light of frequency 6.0 × 10¹⁴ Hz strikes a metal with a work function of 2.5 eV (1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J).

a) Calculate the energy of each photon in joules.

b) Will photoelectrons be emitted? Show your comparison.

c) If yes, calculate the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electrons.

3. [Communication — 3 marks] Explain why the photoelectric effect cannot be explained by the wave model of light. Include: the role of frequency vs. intensity, the concept of a threshold frequency, and how the photon model resolves the problem.

4. [Application — 4 marks] A diffraction grating has 5000 lines per centimetre. White light (400-700 nm) passes through it.

a) Calculate the slit spacing d.

b) Calculate the angle range for the first-order spectrum (m=1).

c) Would the second-order spectra overlap with the first? Explain.
