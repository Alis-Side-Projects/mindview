⚡ Strand B: Energy and Momentum

Course: SPH4U — Physics, Grade 12
Focus: Work, energy, power, momentum, and collisions

1. Work and Energy

W = Fd cosθ   |   E_k = ½mv²   |   E_p = mgh
W_net = ΔE_k   |   P = W/t = Fv
Conservation of Energy: In an isolated system, the total mechanical energy (E_k + E_p) remains constant if only conservative forces act. E_k₁ + E_p₁ = E_k₂ + E_p₂

2. Momentum and Impulse

p = mv   |   J = FΔt = Δp   |   Σp_before = Σp_after

Types of Collisions

TypeMomentum Conserved?Kinetic Energy Conserved?Example
ElasticYesYesBilliard balls (idealized)
InelasticYesNo (some lost)Car crash, clay ball
Perfectly InelasticYesNo (maximum loss)Objects stick together
ExplosionYesNo (KE increases)Firecracker, recoil

📝 Worksheet — Strand B: Energy and Momentum

1. [K/U — 3 marks] A 0.50 kg ball is dropped from a height of 10.0 m.

a) Calculate its potential energy at the top.

b) Using conservation of energy, find its speed just before hitting the ground.

2. [K/U — 3 marks] A 60 kg skater moving at 4.0 m/s collides with and holds onto a stationary 80 kg skater.

a) What type of collision is this?

b) Calculate their combined velocity after the collision.

c) Calculate the kinetic energy lost in the collision.

3. [Thinking — 4 marks] A 2000 kg car traveling east at 20 m/s collides with a 3000 kg truck traveling north at 15 m/s. They lock together.

a) Find the velocity (magnitude and direction) of the wreckage after collision.

b) Was this collision elastic or inelastic? Justify with a calculation.

4. [Communication — 3 marks] Explain why modern cars are designed with crumple zones using the concepts of impulse and momentum. Include the relationship J = FΔt in your explanation.

5. [Application — 4 marks] A roller coaster car (mass 500 kg, including riders) starts from rest at the top of a 40 m hill.

a) What is its speed at the bottom of the hill? (Ignore friction.)

b) The car then enters a vertical loop of radius 10 m. What is the minimum speed needed at the top of the loop to maintain contact?

c) Will the car make it through the loop? Show your reasoning.
